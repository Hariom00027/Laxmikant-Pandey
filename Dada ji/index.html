The Legend

A journey of dedication, strength, and honor

Early Life & Introduction to Wrestling

Born in 1936 in the historic city of Varanasi, Uttar Pradesh, Laxmikant Pandey grew up in Koiripur (Khurd) Post-Ramapur, Hathiwar. His journey into wrestling began during his service in the Uttar Pradesh Police Service, where his natural athleticism and determination caught the attention of his superiors.

Standing at 5'9" with an imposing presence, Laxmikant quickly established himself as a formidable wrestler. His technique, strength, and indomitable spirit earned him the nickname "Chikkan Pehelwan" - a name that would become synonymous with excellence in Indian wrestling.

Family & Personal Life

Laxmikant Pandey was married to Mithila Pandey and was blessed with five children: Prabhu Narayan Pandey, Shri Narayan Pandey, Kapil Narayan Pandey, Neelam Tiwari, and Ratna Chaubey. His brother Paras Nath Pandey also shared his passion for wrestling, creating a family legacy in the sport.

Legacy

Laxmikant Pandey's contribution to Indian wrestling extends beyond his medals. He inspired countless young wrestlers and helped establish India's presence on the international wrestling stage. His dedication to the sport and his country continues to inspire generations of athletes.
